## 1. Definitions

For the purposes of this DPA, the following terms shall have the meanings set out below. Capitalised terms not defined here shall have the meanings given in the Services Agreement.

1.1 **"Applicable Data Protection Law"** means any law, regulation, directive, or binding guideline applicable to the processing of Personal Data under this DPA, including but not limited to: the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"); the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018; the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) and California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA); and any other applicable national, state, or provincial data protection legislation, in each case as amended, supplemented, or replaced from time to time.

1.2 **"Controller"** means the natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data. For the purposes of this DPA, the Customer is the Controller.

1.3 **"Data Breach"** means a breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed by SyncTek LLC.

1.4 **"Data Subject"** means an identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.

1.5 **"EEA"** means the European Economic Area.

1.6 **"Personal Data"** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that SyncTek LLC processes on behalf of Customer in connection with the provision of ForgeOS, as further described in Annex A.

1.7 **"Processing"** (and its cognates, including "process" and "processed") means any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation or alteration, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure by transmission, dissemination or otherwise making available, alignment or combination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.

1.8 **"Processor"** means a natural or legal person, public authority, agency, or other body which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. For the purposes of this DPA, SyncTek LLC is the Processor.

1.9 **"Restricted Transfer"** means a transfer of Personal Data from the EEA, the United Kingdom, or another jurisdiction that restricts international transfers, to a country that has not been granted an adequacy decision by the relevant regulatory authority.

1.10 **"Standard Contractual Clauses"** or **"SCCs"** means the standard contractual clauses for the transfer of personal data to third countries pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2016/679, as adopted by the European Commission by Decision 2021/914 of 4 June 2021 (Module Two: Controller to Processor), as may be updated or replaced from time to time.

1.11 **"Sub-processor"** means any Processor engaged by SyncTek LLC to carry out specific processing activities on behalf of the Customer in connection with ForgeOS.

1.12 **"Supervisory Authority"** means any independent public authority responsible for monitoring the application of Applicable Data Protection Law.

## 2. Scope and Purpose of Processing

2.1 **Subject Matter.** SyncTek LLC shall process Personal Data solely for the purpose of providing the ForgeOS service to Customer, including: generating code intelligence, trust scores, and governance artifacts; operating, maintaining, and improving the ForgeOS platform; providing customer support; and complying with legal obligations applicable to SyncTek LLC as set out in this DPA. Full details of the processing activities are set out in **Annex A**.

2.2 **Controller Instructions.** SyncTek LLC shall process Personal Data only on documented instructions from Customer, including those set out in this DPA and the Services Agreement. Customer's instructions are: (a) to process Personal Data to the extent necessary to provide ForgeOS; and (b) to process Personal Data in accordance with this DPA. Customer may provide additional written instructions from time to time; SyncTek LLC shall promptly notify Customer if it believes any instruction violates Applicable Data Protection Law.

2.3 **Compliance.** Each Party shall comply with its respective obligations under Applicable Data Protection Law. Customer represents and warrants that it has a valid lawful basis for providing Personal Data to SyncTek LLC and for instructing the processing activities contemplated under this DPA.

2.4 **Confidentiality of Processing.** SyncTek LLC shall ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data are under appropriate obligations of confidentiality, whether contractual or statutory.

2.5 **No Sale of Personal Data.** SyncTek LLC shall not sell, rent, lease, or otherwise commercially exploit Personal Data for its own benefit or for the benefit of any third party. SyncTek LLC shall not use Personal Data to develop, train, or improve AI or machine learning models beyond the scope of providing ForgeOS to Customer, except as separately agreed in writing by Customer.

## 3. Customer Obligations

3.1 **Lawful Basis.** Customer shall, at all times during the term of this DPA, ensure that it has a valid and lawful basis under Applicable Data Protection Law for providing Personal Data to SyncTek LLC and for instructing SyncTek LLC to process Personal Data as contemplated by this DPA.

3.2 **Notice to Data Subjects.** Customer shall provide Data Subjects with all required notices and disclosures about the processing of their Personal Data in connection with ForgeOS, including information about the categories of Personal Data processed, the purposes of processing, and the identity of SyncTek LLC as a data processor, as required by Applicable Data Protection Law.

3.3 **Data Subject Requests.** Customer shall be responsible for responding to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Applicable Data Protection Law (including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection). SyncTek LLC shall, upon Customer's reasonable written request, provide such technical and organisational assistance as is necessary and reasonably practicable to assist Customer in responding to such requests, taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to SyncTek LLC.

3.4 **Data Accuracy.** Customer shall ensure that Personal Data provided to SyncTek LLC is accurate, complete, and limited to what is necessary for the purposes of providing ForgeOS.

3.5 **Applicable Law.** Customer shall comply with all Applicable Data Protection Law with respect to its collection, transfer, and other processing of Personal Data prior to providing it to SyncTek LLC.

## 4. Technical and Organisational Security Measures

4.1 **Obligation to Implement Measures.** SyncTek LLC shall implement and maintain appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect Personal Data against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, or access ("**Security Measures**"). Such measures shall ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of processing.

4.2 **Description of Measures.** The Security Measures currently implemented by SyncTek LLC are described in **Annex C**. SyncTek LLC may update the Security Measures from time to time provided that such updates do not materially reduce the overall level of protection afforded to Personal Data.

4.3 **Personnel.** SyncTek LLC shall take reasonable steps to ensure that persons with authorised access to Personal Data: (a) are aware of the confidential nature of the Personal Data; (b) have received appropriate data protection training; and (c) are subject to binding confidentiality obligations.

4.4 **Security Assessments.** SyncTek LLC shall conduct regular security assessments and penetration testing of systems used to process Personal Data, and shall promptly remediate identified vulnerabilities in accordance with risk severity.

## 5. Sub-processors

5.1 **Authorised Sub-processors.** Customer acknowledges and agrees that SyncTek LLC may engage the Sub-processors listed in **Annex B** to assist in the provision of ForgeOS. SyncTek LLC shall impose data protection terms on each Sub-processor that are no less protective than those imposed on SyncTek LLC under this DPA.

5.2 **Prior Notice of Changes.** SyncTek LLC shall provide Customer with at least **30 days' prior written notice** before: (a) appointing a new Sub-processor; or (b) making material changes to an existing Sub-processor engagement that affects the processing of Personal Data. Notice may be provided by email to the address on record for Customer's account, or by update to a maintained sub-processor list at synctek.io/legal/sub-processors.

5.3 **Customer Right to Object.** Customer may object to a new or changed Sub-processor engagement on reasonable data protection grounds by providing written notice to legal@synctek.io within **15 days** of receipt of the notice described in Section 5.2. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith to resolve the objection. If the Parties are unable to resolve the objection within 30 days, Customer may, as its sole remedy, terminate the affected services upon 30 days' written notice, subject to the termination provisions of the Services Agreement.

5.4 **Liability for Sub-processors.** SyncTek LLC shall remain liable to Customer for the acts and omissions of its Sub-processors to the same extent as SyncTek LLC would be liable if performing the processing directly, subject to any limitations of liability in the Services Agreement.

## 6. Data Breach Notification

6.1 **SyncTek LLC Obligations.** Upon becoming aware of a confirmed Data Breach affecting Personal Data, SyncTek LLC shall:

  (a) notify Customer without undue delay, and in any event within **72 hours** of confirming the Data Breach;

  (b) provide Customer, to the extent then known and reasonably practicable, with the following information:
  - the nature of the Data Breach, including the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects affected and the categories and approximate volume of Personal Data records affected;
  - the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point from whom further information may be obtained;
  - the likely consequences of the Data Breach; and
  - the measures taken or proposed to be taken by SyncTek LLC to address the Data Breach, including measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects;

  (c) cooperate with Customer and take such reasonable steps as are requested by Customer to assist in the investigation, mitigation, and remediation of the Data Breach; and

  (d) not make any public disclosures regarding the Data Breach that reference Customer without Customer's prior written consent, except as required by applicable law.

6.2 **Notification Threshold.** SyncTek LLC's obligation to notify under Section 6.1 shall be triggered upon SyncTek LLC's reasonable confirmation of a Data Breach. Notification does not constitute an acknowledgement of fault or liability.

6.3 **Customer Reporting Obligations.** Customer is solely responsible for determining whether to notify any Supervisory Authority, Data Subjects, or other third parties of a Data Breach, as required by Applicable Data Protection Law, and SyncTek LLC shall provide reasonable assistance to Customer in fulfilling such obligations.

## 7. Audit Rights

7.1 **Right to Audit.** Upon Customer's written request, SyncTek LLC shall make available to Customer, or Customer's designated auditor bound by confidentiality obligations no less restrictive than those in this DPA, such information as is reasonably necessary to demonstrate SyncTek LLC's compliance with its obligations under this DPA.

7.2 **Audit Frequency and Notice.** Customer may exercise its audit rights under this Section 7 no more than **once per 12-month period**, unless: (a) Customer has reasonable grounds to suspect a material Data Breach; or (b) a Supervisory Authority requires an audit. Customer shall provide SyncTek LLC with at least **30 days' prior written notice** of its intent to conduct an audit, specifying the scope, method, and proposed start date.

7.3 **SOC 2 and Equivalents.** SyncTek LLC may satisfy all or part of its obligations under this Section 7 by providing Customer with an up-to-date SOC 2 Type II report, ISO 27001 certification, or equivalent third-party audit report covering the systems and processes used to process Personal Data ("**Audit Report**"). Where an Audit Report is provided, Customer's right to conduct an on-site audit under Section 7.1 shall be limited to matters not covered by the Audit Report or where Customer has reasonable grounds to believe the Audit Report is insufficient.

7.4 **Costs.** Audits shall be conducted at Customer's expense. SyncTek LLC may charge Customer a reasonable fee to recover costs associated with making information available and facilitating audits.

7.5 **Confidentiality of Findings.** Customer shall treat all information obtained during any audit as confidential and shall not disclose such information to any third party without SyncTek LLC's prior written consent, except as required by applicable law or Supervisory Authority.

## 8. International Data Transfers

8.1 **Primary Processing Location.** Personal Data processed by SyncTek LLC in connection with ForgeOS is primarily processed in the United States. SyncTek LLC may also process Personal Data in other countries where its Sub-processors operate, as listed in Annex B.

8.2 **Restricted Transfers — SCCs.** To the extent that Customer's use of ForgeOS involves a Restricted Transfer of Personal Data from the EEA or the United Kingdom to the United States or another country not subject to an adequacy decision, the Parties agree that the SCCs are hereby incorporated into this DPA by reference and shall apply to such transfers, as follows:

  (a) **EU SCCs:** The European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (Decision 2021/914, Module Two: Controller to Processor) shall apply. The Annex I, Annex II, and Annex III information required by the SCCs shall be as set out in Annex A and Annex C of this DPA, supplemented as necessary by the Parties' written agreement.

  (b) **UK Transfers:** For transfers from the United Kingdom, the applicable UK addendum to the SCCs (as issued by the UK Information Commissioner's Office) shall apply, incorporating this DPA as the "Approved Addendum."

8.3 **Transfer Impact Assessment.** SyncTek LLC shall, upon Customer's written request, make available a transfer impact assessment for the relevant data flows, to assist Customer in determining whether appropriate safeguards are in place.

8.4 **Adequacy.** To the extent that an adequacy decision is adopted for the United States or another relevant destination country under Applicable Data Protection Law after the date of this DPA, the relevant adequacy mechanism shall replace the SCCs for transfers to that country, upon mutual written agreement of the Parties.

## 9. Data Retention and Deletion

9.1 **Retention During Services.** SyncTek LLC shall retain Personal Data only for as long as necessary to provide ForgeOS and as required by applicable law.

9.2 **Deletion on Termination.** Upon termination or expiry of the Services Agreement, or upon Customer's earlier written request, SyncTek LLC shall, at Customer's election, either: (a) securely delete all Personal Data in SyncTek LLC's possession or control; or (b) return all Personal Data to Customer in a portable format — in each case within **30 days** of the effective date of termination or receipt of Customer's request.

9.3 **Certification.** Upon Customer's written request following deletion under Section 9.2, SyncTek LLC shall provide Customer with a written certification confirming deletion or return of Personal Data.

9.4 **Legal Retention Obligations.** Notwithstanding Section 9.2, SyncTek LLC may retain Personal Data to the extent and for the period required by applicable law, provided that SyncTek LLC shall: (a) notify Customer of any such legal obligation before deletion (unless prohibited by law); (b) treat retained data as confidential; and (c) delete such data as soon as the retention obligation ceases.

9.5 **Sub-processors.** SyncTek LLC shall ensure that its Sub-processors comply with deletion obligations equivalent to those in this Section 9.

## 10. Assistance to Customer

10.1 **Data Subject Rights.** Taking into account the nature of the processing, SyncTek LLC shall provide reasonable technical and organisational assistance to Customer in fulfilling Customer's obligations to respond to requests by Data Subjects to exercise their rights under Applicable Data Protection Law, including rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction, data portability, and objection.

10.2 **Data Protection Impact Assessments.** To the extent required by Applicable Data Protection Law, SyncTek LLC shall provide reasonable assistance to Customer in conducting data protection impact assessments and, where required, in consulting with Supervisory Authorities, in relation to processing activities covered by this DPA.

10.3 **Regulatory Inquiries.** SyncTek LLC shall promptly notify Customer of any inquiry, investigation, complaint, or order received from a Supervisory Authority relating to the processing of Customer's Personal Data, and shall cooperate with Customer in responding to such matter, to the extent permitted by law.

## 11. Liability

11.1 **General Liability.** Each Party shall be liable to the other Party for any direct losses, damages, costs, and expenses arising from or in connection with that Party's breach of its obligations under this DPA, subject to the limitations of liability set out in the Services Agreement.

11.2 **Liability Cap.** The aggregate liability of each Party under or in connection with this DPA, whether arising in contract, tort (including negligence), breach of statutory duty, or otherwise, shall not exceed the aggregate liability cap specified in the Services Agreement.

11.3 **No Exclusion of Mandatory Rights.** Nothing in this DPA shall limit either Party's liability for: (a) fraud or fraudulent misrepresentation; (b) death or personal injury caused by negligence; or (c) any other liability that cannot be excluded or limited by applicable law.

11.4 **Third-Party Claims.** Where a Data Subject or Supervisory Authority brings a claim against one Party in respect of a breach for which both Parties are responsible, each Party shall be liable for the portion of the loss attributable to its own breach, and shall indemnify the other Party accordingly.

## 12. Term and Termination

12.1 **Term.** This DPA shall take effect on the date of execution and shall continue in force for the duration of the Services Agreement, unless terminated earlier in accordance with this DPA.

12.2 **Termination.** This DPA shall automatically terminate upon expiry or termination of the Services Agreement for any reason.

12.3 **Survival.** Sections 4, 6, 7, 9, 10, and 11 of this DPA shall survive termination or expiry of this DPA for as long as SyncTek LLC retains any Personal Data or as otherwise required by applicable law.

## 13. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

13.1 **Governing Law.** This DPA shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of Delaware, United States, without regard to its conflict of laws principles, except to the extent that mandatory provisions of Applicable Data Protection Law (including the GDPR) require a different governing law with respect to specific obligations.

13.2 **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising out of or in connection with this DPA shall be resolved in accordance with the dispute resolution provisions of the Services Agreement.

13.3 **Supervisory Authority.** Nothing in this DPA shall prevent either Party from lodging a complaint with, or cooperating with, a competent Supervisory Authority.

## 14. Miscellaneous

14.1 **Entire Agreement.** Together with the Services Agreement, this DPA constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter of data processing and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, and representations relating to that subject matter.

14.2 **Amendments.** SyncTek LLC may amend this DPA from time to time to reflect changes in Applicable Data Protection Law or to incorporate updated SCCs. SyncTek LLC shall provide Customer with at least 30 days' written notice of material amendments. Customer's continued use of ForgeOS after the effective date of an amendment constitutes acceptance of the amended DPA, unless Customer notifies SyncTek LLC of an objection in writing prior to the effective date.

14.3 **Severability.** If any provision of this DPA is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect, and the Parties shall negotiate in good faith to replace the invalid or unenforceable provision with a valid provision that achieves, as closely as possible, the same legal and commercial effect.

14.4 **Waiver.** No failure or delay by either Party in exercising any right under this DPA shall operate as a waiver of that right.

14.5 **Counterparts.** This DPA may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute a single instrument. Electronic signatures shall be deemed valid.

14.6 **Notices.** All notices under this DPA shall be in writing and delivered to the contact details set out at the beginning of this DPA, or such updated details as either Party provides in writing from time to time.

## Annex C: Technical and Organisational Security Measures

This Annex describes the technical and organisational security measures implemented by SyncTek LLC as of the Effective Date of this DPA, in accordance with Section 4 of this DPA. SyncTek LLC may update these measures from time to time; updates that materially reduce the level of protection will require prior written notice to Customer.

---

### C.1 Encryption

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Encryption in transit** | TLS 1.2 or higher required for all data transmissions between clients and the ForgeOS API and between ForgeOS and its sub-processors |
| **Encryption at rest** | All persistent data stores encrypted using AES-256 encryption |
| **Key management** | Encryption keys managed using industry-standard key management practices; keys stored separately from encrypted data; regular key rotation |
| **Cryptographic audit trail integrity** | Ed25519 elliptic curve digital signatures applied to all audit trail records and governance ledger entries, providing tamper-evident integrity verification |

---

### C.2 Access Controls

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Authentication** | Multi-factor authentication (MFA) required for all administrative and privileged access to systems processing Personal Data |
| **Role-based access control (RBAC)** | Access to Personal Data granted on a role-based, least-privilege basis; permissions reviewed quarterly |
| **Privileged access management** | Privileged account access logged, monitored, and subject to additional authentication requirements |
| **Account lifecycle management** | Access credentials revoked promptly upon personnel departure or role change |
| **API authentication** | All API endpoints require authenticated sessions via token-based authentication; tokens are time-limited and scoped |

---

### C.3 Network and Infrastructure Security

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Network segmentation** | Production systems logically separated from development and staging environments |
| **Firewall and perimeter controls** | Web application firewall (WAF) and DDoS protection implemented via Cloudflare |
| **Intrusion detection** | Network and application-level monitoring for anomalous activity |
| **Vulnerability management** | Regular automated vulnerability scanning; critical and high vulnerabilities remediated within defined SLAs |
| **Penetration testing** | Annual third-party penetration testing of production systems |
| **Patch management** | Operating systems, libraries, and dependencies subject to regular patching; critical security patches applied within 72 hours of release |

---

### C.4 Data Protection and Privacy by Design

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Data minimisation** | Only Personal Data necessary for the stated processing purposes is collected and retained |
| **Pseudonymisation** | Where technically feasible, Personal Data is pseudonymised within analytics and logging pipelines |
| **Data retention controls** | Automated retention policies enforce deletion of Personal Data in accordance with defined retention schedules |
| **Privacy impact assessments** | Privacy impact assessments conducted for new processing activities or significant changes to existing processing |

---

### C.5 Physical Security

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Data centre security** | Personal Data hosted on cloud infrastructure (Railway, Google Cloud) subject to those providers' physical security controls, including ISO 27001 and SOC 2 certification |
| **Office security** | SyncTek LLC office premises protected by access controls; no Personal Data stored on unencrypted physical media |

---

### C.6 Organisational Measures

| Measure | Implementation |
|---------|---------------|
| **Data protection training** | All personnel with access to Personal Data receive data protection and security awareness training upon joining and annually thereafter |
| **Confidentiality obligations** | All personnel with access to Personal Data bound by contractual confidentiality obligations |
| **Incident response plan** | Documented data breach response procedure, reviewed annually, with defined escalation paths and notification timelines |
| **Supplier management** | Third-party sub-processors assessed for data protection compliance prior to engagement and subject to data processing agreements |

---

### C.7 Monitoring and Logging

| Measure | Implementation |
